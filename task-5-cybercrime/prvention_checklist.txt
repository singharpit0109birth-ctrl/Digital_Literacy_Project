Stay Safe Online: Cybercrime Prevention Checklist for College Students
Created by: Jadhav Radhesham (25BCE11168)

1. NEVER enter your UPI PIN to receive money. Your PIN is only used when you are sending money to someone or checking your balance.
2. Always verify the receiver's name and the amount displayed on your UPI app before completing any transaction.
3. Do not scan random QR codes sent by unknown buyers or sellers over WhatsApp or email.
4. Use strong, unique passwords for your college portal, personal email, and social media accounts.
5. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all your important accounts to add an extra layer of security.
6. Avoid using free or public Wi-Fi networks (like at cafes or railway stations) when making financial transactions.
7. Keep your smartphone and laptop software up to date to protect against new security threats.
8. Reporting Channel: If you or your friend becomes a victim of online fraud, immediately call the National Cyber Crime Helpline at 1930. You can also file a complaint online at the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in).
