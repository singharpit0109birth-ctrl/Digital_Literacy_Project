Cybercrime Case Study: UPI Payment Request Fraud
Written by: Jadhav Radhesham (25BCE11168)

What is the crime?
UPI Payment Request Fraud is a financial scam where fraudsters trick victims into entering their UPI PIN to "receive" money. Instead, the money is instantly deducted from the victim's bank account.

How it typically happens (Step-by-Step):
1. The scammer contacts a student who has listed an old item (like a bicycle or textbook) for sale on an online platform.
2. The scammer immediately agrees to the asking price to build trust and says they will send the money online via GPay or PhonePe.
3. Instead of sending money, the scammer sends a "Payment Request" link or a QR code to the student's WhatsApp.
4. The scammer calls the student and creates a sense of extreme urgency, saying, "I have sent the money, quickly enter your UPI PIN to receive it."
5. Confused and in a hurry, the student enters their secret PIN. The requested amount is immediately deducted from their account.

Who is targeted?
College students selling second-hand items, elderly individuals, and people who are new to digital payments are the most common targets.

Consequences:
The immediate consequence is sudden financial loss. For college students, losing pocket money or semester fee savings causes severe mental stress. Recovering this money is also a very long and difficult process.
